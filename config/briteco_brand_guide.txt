BRITECO EDITORIAL STYLE GUIDE — APPLY TO ALL WRITING

NUMBERS
- Use the percent symbol (%) rather than typing out 'percent'
  Correct: Our survey found that 71% of jewelers believed earrings to be the most popular item.
  Incorrect: Our survey found that seventy-one percent of jewelers believed earrings to be the most popular item.
- For ages, use numbers rather than spelling out
  Correct: She is 58-years-old
  Incorrect: She is fifty-eight-years-old
- For zero, spell out rather than using a number
  Correct: We offer zero deductibles
  Incorrect: We offer $0 deductibles
- For phone number, only provide BriteCo's main number: (312) 809-9100

ABBREVIATIONS
- For country abbreviations, do not use periods
  Correct: US, UK
  Incorrect: U.S., U.K.
- For Washington, DC, do not use periods
  Correct: Washington, DC
  Incorrect: Washington, D.C.

PUNCTUATION
- Use a serial comma when writing out a list
  Correct: Our jewelry insurance covers theft, loss, and damages.
  Incorrect: Our jewelry insurance covers theft, loss and damages.
- Use an em dash when offsetting copy in a sentence (not a hyphen) with spaces around it
  Correct: Engagement rings — while pricey — are a worthwhile investment.
  Incorrect: Engagement rings—while pricey—are a worthwhile investment.
  Incorrect: Engagement rings - while pricey - are a worthwhile investment.
- Put names of movies, books, and periodicals in italics, not quotes
  Correct: Our CEO recently published a new expert article on Forbes.
  Incorrect: Our CEO recently published a new expert article on "Forbes."
- When you have a quote within a sentence, put punctuation inside the quotation marks
  Correct: Our tagline is, "Modern protection for modern milestones."
  Incorrect: Our tagline is, "Modern protection for modern milestones".
- Use a hyphen between two words modifying a noun
  Example: We are proud of our 5-star ratings.
  Example: Our insurance offers budget-friendly policies.
- Always hyphenate lab-grown

TITLE CASE VS. SENTENCE CASE
- Headlines (H1s, H2s, H3s): use title case — every major word capitalized except prepositions/conjunctions
  Example: Types of Flower Arrangements You'll See at Most Weddings
- All other copy (body, bullet lists): use sentence case

BRAND NAMES
- Omega Watches (not OMEGA Watches)

BRITECO BRAND RULES
- DO call BriteCo an 'insurtech company' or an 'insurance provider'
- DON'T call BriteCo an 'insurance company'
- DO refer to BriteCo as a specialty jewelry insurance provider when comparing to general insurers
- DON'T refer to BriteCo as 'specialized jewelry insurance'
- DON'T slander competitors or link to them
- DO say we are 'backed by an AM Best A+ rated Insurance Carrier'
- DON'T say 'we have AM Best policies' or 'we are AM Best'
- DO refer to the website as brite.co or https://brite.co
- DON'T refer to the website as www.brite.co
- DO spell it stand-alone (not standalone)

TONE — WEDDING INSURANCE
- Be inclusive, use gender-less verbiage (they, them, theirs)
- Use 'wedding party' rather than 'bridal party'
- Say 'groom crew' over 'groomsmen'
- Rather than bride and groom, say partner, soon-to-be spouse, etc.

TONE — JEWELRY
- When mentioning health claims or benefits, never promise results; use softer language
  Correct: Copper may be helpful with boosting immune function.
  Incorrect: Wearing copper will increase immune function.

GLOSSARY
- Homeowners policy (not Homeowner's or Homeowners')
- Renters insurance (not Renter's or Renters')
- Stand Alone (not standalone or stand-alone)
