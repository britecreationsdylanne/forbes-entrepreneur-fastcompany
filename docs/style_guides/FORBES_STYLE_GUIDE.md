# Forbes Business Council Style Guide
## Dustin Lemick Article Standards

---

## Overview

Forbes Business Council articles are **reflective thought leadership pieces** that explore business concepts with nuance and personal insight. The tone is advisory and philosophical rather than prescriptive.

---

## Specifications

| Element | Range |
|---------|-------|
| **Word Count** | 750-1,100 words |
| **Character Count** | 4,500-6,500 characters |
| **Typical Length** | ~850 words |

---

## Article Formats

### 1. Philosophical (Most Common)
**Use for:** Exploring nuanced business concepts, challenging conventional wisdom

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Hook with famous quote, fable, or common saying
- **Body:** 3 themed sections with phrase-based subheadings
- **Conclusion:** Nuanced wrap-up paragraph (no explicit header)

**Word Count:** 750-850 words

**Example Subheadings:**
- "There's a very fine line."
- "Imitation is the best form of flattery."
- "Know when it's time to work together."

---

### 2. Tactical (Numbered Lists)
**Use for:** Actionable how-to guides, step-by-step advice

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Context setting with personal experience
- **Body:** Numbered list (1. 2. 3. 4. 5.) with explanations
- **Conclusion:** "Final Thoughts" section

**Word Count:** 1,000-1,100 words

**Example Subheadings:**
- "1. Full-team meetings are important."
- "2. Teams can grow each others' knowledge."
- "3. Everyone's role is everyone's business."

---

### 3. Personal Narrative
**Use for:** Founder journey, lessons learned, vulnerable insights

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Personal admission or struggle
- **Body:** 5-7 short sections with phrase subheadings
- **Conclusion:** Reassuring message about growth

**Word Count:** 900-1,000 words

**Example Subheadings:**
- "Learning To Let Go"
- "Creating Space For The Team"
- "The Fundraising Grind"
- "Finding Joy In The Work"

---

### 4. Educational Guide
**Use for:** Phase-based instruction, business stage guidance

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Relatable founder struggle
- **Body:** Phase-based sections (Startup Phase, Growth Phase)
- **Conclusion:** "Overview" section

**Word Count:** 850-950 words

## Intro Styles

| Style | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Famous Quote** | Open with business leader quote | Mark Zuckerberg's "move fast and break things" |
| **Classic Fable** | Reference well-known story | Tortoise and the Hare |
| **Common Saying** | Start with adage, validate/challenge | "There's strength in numbers" |
| **Statistic** | Compelling data point (rare) | "According to Cornell, 77%..." |
| **Personal Admission** | Vulnerable statement | "I admit I wasn't great at..." |

---

## Subheading Style

**Format:** Phrase-based, sentence case (NOT all caps)

**Characteristics:**
- Often idioms or common expressions
- Sometimes wordplay/puns ("Always brake for customers")
- Action-oriented but not clickbait
- 3-8 words typically

**Good Examples:**
- ✅ "Pace yourself."
- ✅ "Find the right balance."
- ✅ "Know your place and act accordingly."

**Avoid:**
- ❌ ALL CAPS HEADERS
- ❌ Clickbait phrases
- ❌ Questions as subheadings

---

## Tone & Voice

### Tone
- **Primary:** Reflective, advisory, honest
- **Secondary:** Philosophical, nuanced, balanced
- **Avoid:** Preachy, absolute, salesy, overly formal

### Voice
- First person throughout
- Key phrases:
  - "In my experience..."
  - "I've learned that..."
  - "At BriteCo, we..."
  - "What I've discovered..."
  - "Looking back..."

---

## BriteCo Integration

**Frequency:** 2-4 mentions woven throughout

**Types of References:**
- Company practices and programs
- Team examples (underwriting team, etc.)
- Founding story elements
- Industry context (insurance, jewelry, regulated industry)

**Example Phrases:**
- "At BriteCo, our underwriting team..."
- "We have an unlimited vacation policy..."
- "My company encourages employees to..."
- "In a regulated industry like insurance..."

---

## Required vs Optional Elements

### Required
- ✅ Metaphors or analogies
- ✅ Personal experience reference
- ✅ Balanced/nuanced conclusion
- ✅ BriteCo reference

### Optional
- Statistics (rare, only when relevant)
- Famous quotes (common in intros)
- Other company case studies

---

## What to Avoid

- ❌ Hard statistics throughout (observation-based preferred)
- ❌ Black and white conclusions ("always" or "never")
- ❌ Overly prescriptive tone
- ❌ Clickbait subheadings
- ❌ ALL CAPS formatting
- ❌ Competitor bashing
- ❌ Political statements
- ❌ Personnel news

## Headline Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| How [Topic] + Question | "How Much Should You Focus On Competitors To Stay Ahead?" |
| Why [Concept] Isn't/Is [Insight] | "Why Growth Isn't Just About Speed; It's About Stability" |
| How To [Action] + [Benefit] | "How To Structure Cross-Department Collaboration" |
| [Topic]: Lessons From [Experience] | "Navigating Burnout As A Startup Leader: Lessons From The Trenches" |
| From [A] To [B]: How To | "From Family Dynamics To Business Success: How To Succeed..." |

## Sample Article Structure

```
HEADLINE: How [Concept] Can [Benefit] Your Business

INTRO (2-3 paragraphs):
- Hook: Famous quote, fable, or saying
- Context: Why this matters now
- Thesis: What you'll explore

SECTION 1: [Idiom-based subheading]
- Key insight
- BriteCo example
- Broader application

SECTION 2: [Phrase-based subheading]
- Second insight
- Supporting evidence
- Practical takeaway

SECTION 3: [Action-oriented subheading]
- Third insight
- Personal experience
- Forward-looking advice

CONCLUSION (1-2 paragraphs):
- Nuanced summary
- Balanced perspective
- Call to reflection (not action)
```
