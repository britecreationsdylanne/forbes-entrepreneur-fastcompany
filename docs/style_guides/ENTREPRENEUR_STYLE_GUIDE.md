# Entrepreneur Style Guide
## Dustin Lemick Article Standards

---

## Overview

Entrepreneur articles are **provocative, practical thought leadership** that challenges conventional business wisdom and provides actionable insights. The tone is more direct and assertive than Forbes.

---

## Specifications

| Element | Range |
|---------|-------|
| **Word Count** | 800-900 words |
| **Character Count** | 4,800-5,400 characters |
| **Typical Length** | ~850 words |

---

## Required Element: Key Takeaways

**Every Entrepreneur article MUST include a Key Takeaways box at the top.**

**Format:**
- 3 bullet points
- Concise, action-oriented statements
- Summarize main insights

**Examples:**
```
Key Takeaways
• Mistakes are inevitable — it's how you respond that shapes your company's culture.
• Don't launch without research — timing beats speed every time.
• Work-life balance is a myth; focus instead on intentional work-life integration.
```

---

## Article Formats

### 1. Sectioned (Most Common)
**Use for:** Challenging conventional wisdom, providing structured insights

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Contrarian hook challenging common belief
- **Key Takeaways:** 3 bullets at top
- **Body:** 3-4 sections with phrase-based subheadings
- **Conclusion:** Action-oriented wrap-up (no explicit header)

**Word Count:** 800-850 words

**Example Subheadings:**
- "It's not one or the other — It's both"
- "Making work meaningful"
- "What a good post-mortem looks like"
- "Consider pre-mortems, too"

---

### 2. Numbered List
**Use for:** Actionable tips, things to know, step-by-step guides

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Context and why this matters
- **Key Takeaways:** 3 bullets at top
- **Body:** Numbered sections (1. 2. 3. 4. 5.)
- **Conclusion:** Final tip serves as natural wrap

**Word Count:** 850-900 words

**Headline Pattern:** "X Things/Ways to [Action]"

**Example Subheadings:**
- "1. Engage in market research first"
- "2. Be mindful of your core products"
- "3. Ensure you have all the right resources"

---

### 3. Flowing Narrative
**Use for:** Deep thought pieces, complex topics requiring exploration

**Structure:**
- **Intro:** Problem statement or observation
- **Key Takeaways:** 3 bullets at top
- **Body:** Flowing paragraphs (no section headers)
- **Conclusion:** Call to action or reflection

**Word Count:** 850-900 words

**Note:** "Related:" links are interspersed by editors

---

## Headline Patterns

| Pattern | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Contrarian + Em-Dash** | [Statement] — Here's [Why/How] | "Annual Reviews Are Lazy — Here's How to Actually Learn" |
| **Statistic Hook** | [X%] of [Group] — Here's How | "58% of College Grads Are Still Job Hunting — Here's How..." |
| **Numbered List** | X Things/Ways to [Action] | "5 Things to Know Before Launching a New Product" |
| **How To** | How to [Action] [Qualifier] | "How to Hire the Right Employees Who Will Grow..." |
| **Why Statement** | Why [Topic] [Insight] | "Why Your Business Won't Scale Without Expert Advisors" |

### Headline Characteristics
- Often uses **em-dash (—)** as a pivot
- Frequently **contrarian** or **myth-busting**
- **Statistics** make strong hooks
- More **provocative** than Forbes

## Intro Styles

### Contrarian Challenge (Most Common)
Challenge a commonly held belief:
> "We've seen all the think pieces about work-life balance... But what if none of it is real?"

> "It's that time of year again... I always have one question: Why do we do this just once a year?"

### Statistic Hook
Open with compelling data:
> "According to Fortune, more than half of new college graduates — 58% — are still looking for a job."

### Personal Revelation
Share insight or secret:
> "I'm going to divulge one of the biggest secrets to building your business."

### Cultural Reference
Pop culture or current events:
> "If you haven't watched the latest season of 'The Bear' yet, I highly recommend it..."

## Tone & Voice

### Tone
- **Primary:** Provocative, practical, direct
- **Secondary:** Challenging, actionable, confident
- **Avoid:** Wishy-washy, overly academic, passive

### Voice Characteristics
- More assertive than Forbes
- Willing to challenge norms
- Personal anecdotes common
- Direct recommendations

### Key Phrases
- "Here's what I've learned"
- "The truth is"
- "But here's the thing"
- "I'll admit"
- "My advice is"
- "At BriteCo, we"

## BriteCo Integration

**Frequency:** 2-5 specific mentions throughout

### Types of References

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Named Programs** | Brite Shadow, Brite Room Spark Sessions |
| **Founding Story** | "When I started BriteCo in 2019..." |
| **Team Practices** | Book club, All Hands functions |
| **Policies** | Unlimited PTO, hybrid work |
| **Products** | Jewelry insurance → wedding insurance |
| **Hiring** | Intern-to-hire pipeline |

### Example Integrations
- "When I started BriteCo in 2019, I thought a lot about what our company culture should be."
- "Our company offers programs like Brite Shadow (to tag along with an employee in a different department)."
- "At BriteCo, we have an in-office book club and host regular All Hands functions."
- "With my company, BriteCo, we began with jewelry insurance, and a new product focused on wedding insurance."

## Recurring Themes (Dustin's Signatures)

### Concepts to Incorporate
- **First-principle thinking** - Not relying on biases, starting from square one
- **Cultural fit** - Paramount in hiring decisions
- **Mission-driven employees** - "Part of the wolf pack"
- **Work-life integration** - Not "balance" but "mastery"
- **Continuous learning** - Post-mortems and pre-mortems
- **Regulated industry** - Insurance context

### Recurring Metaphors
- **Wolf pack** (team unity)
- **Flywheel** (business momentum)

## ELN Submission Guidelines

**Platform:** Entrepreneur Leadership Network (ELN)
**Editorial Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for premium members
**Frequency:** 1-2 articles per month recommended

### Content Rules
- Content must be **100% original and previously unpublished**
- Advice-driven articles sharing experiences and problem-solving techniques
- **No interviews or quotes** from non-household name business leaders/experts
- No overly promotional content — articles bolster credibility, not promote
- No paid links or mentions — no editorial coverage in exchange for compensation
- **No ChatGPT/AI-generated text** — articles are run through AI detector
- No plagiarism or copyright infringement
- No photos — leave all imagery to editors
- Proofread and spell-check before submitting
- Can repost on own website/blog **10 days after publication** with link back to original

### Link Policy
- Only link **a few words** at a time (not whole phrases)
- Cite reputable publications, not blogs
- Can link to your own blog **maximum one time** per story (editors may remove)
- For statistics, link to **the original study**, not another article citing it
- **Do NOT link to Forbes, Fast Company, or Inc.** (competitors)
- Put in your own hyperlinks — do not put URLs in parentheses

### Conflicts of Interest
- Discouraged from writing about anything with financial relationship
- If mentioning friends/business partners, check with editor first and disclose in story

## ELN Style Rules (Overrides)

**IMPORTANT:** These override the BriteCo brand guide where they conflict.

| Rule | Entrepreneur Style |
|------|-------------------|
| **Serial comma** | **NO** (apples, oranges and bananas) |
| **Em dashes** | Space on either side |
| **Quotes** | Commas/periods ALWAYS inside end quote marks |
| **Double spaces** | None after periods |
| **Single quotes** | Only for quotes within quotes and headlines/subheads |
| **Formatting** | No bold, no italics (except publication titles), no all-caps |
| **Subheads** | At least 2 per article, Heading 2, lowercase sentence case, no period, parallel structure |
| **Paragraphs** | 2-3 sentences ideal, one-sentence paragraphs sparingly |
| **Pronouns** | Consistent throughout — don't mix we/I/he/she/you |
| **Company pronouns** | Company is "it" not "they" — use "its" not "their" |
| **Numbers** | Under 10 written out (except %), 10+ as numerals, use % sign, "more than" not "over" |
| **Names** | Full name first reference, last name only after, never first name only |
| **Jargon** | Spell out on first reference with abbreviation in parentheses |
| **Evergreen** | Keep content useful indefinitely — avoid dating to specific moment |

## What to Avoid

- ❌ Passive voice
- ❌ Hedging language ("might," "perhaps," "could be")
- ❌ Over-qualifying statements
- ❌ ALL CAPS subheadings
- ❌ Clickbait without substance
- ❌ Generic advice without BriteCo examples
- ❌ Wishy-washy conclusions
- ❌ **Serial comma** (Entrepreneur does NOT use it)
- ❌ Linking to Forbes, Fast Company, or Inc. (competitors)
- ❌ AI-generated text (run through AI detector)
- ❌ Interviews/quotes from non-household name figures
- ❌ Bold text, italics (except publication titles), all-caps words
- ❌ Double spaces after periods
- ❌ Inconsistent pronouns or verb tenses
- ❌ URLs in parentheses instead of embedded hyperlinks

## Sample Article Structure

```
HEADLINE: [Contrarian Statement] — Here's [How/Why]

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
• First key insight (action-oriented)
• Second key insight (practical)
• Third key insight (forward-looking)

INTRO (2-3 paragraphs):
- Contrarian hook challenging common belief
- Personal connection or statistic
- Transition to main argument

SECTION 1: [Phrase-based subheading]
- Challenge the norm
- BriteCo example
- Practical insight

Related: [Editor adds relevant article]

SECTION 2: [Phrase-based subheading]
- Second key point
- Supporting evidence or story
- Actionable takeaway

SECTION 3: [Phrase-based subheading]
- Third insight
- Forward-looking perspective
- Personal experience

CONCLUSION (1 paragraph):
- Strong action-oriented statement
- Call to change behavior/thinking
- No "Final Thoughts" header needed
```
